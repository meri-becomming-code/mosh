In this section we will solve non-right triangles for missing angles and sides using trigonometric formulas called the Law of Sines and the Law of Cosines. (Section 10.1) Law of Sines Law of Sines Given a triangle with angles and sides opposite labeled as shown, the ratio of sine of angle to length of the opposite side will always be equal, or, symbolically, $$\frac{sin(A)}{a} = \frac{sin(B)}{b} = \frac{sin(C)}{c}$$ Examples: 1. Sketch a triangle \(\triangle ABC\) with \(\angle B = 29^\circ\), \(\angle C = 51^\circ\) and side \(b = 44\). Find the missing information.
Solution $$ \angle A = 180^\circ - 29^\circ - 51^\circ = 100^\circ$$ $$ \frac{sin B}{b} = \frac{sin A}{a}$$ $$ \frac{sin 29^\circ}{44} = \frac{sin 100^\circ}{a}$$ Cross multiply $$ a \cdot sin(29^\circ) = 44 \cdot sin(100^\circ)$$ $$ \frac{a \cdot sin(29^\circ)}{sin(29^\circ)} = \frac{44 \cdot sin(100^\circ)}{sin(29^\circ)} \approx 89.38$$ $$ \frac{sin B}{b} = \frac{sin C}{c}$$ $$ \frac{sin 29^\circ}{44} = \frac{sin 51^\circ}{c}$$ $$ c \cdot sin(29^\circ) = 44 \cdot sin(51^\circ)$$ $$ \frac{c \cdot sin(29^\circ)}{sin(29^\circ)} = \frac{44 \cdot sin(51^\circ)}{sin(29^\circ)}$$ $$ c \approx 70.53$$
2. To find the distance across a river, a surveyor chooses points A and B, which are 200ft apart on one side of the river. She then chooses a reference point C on the opposite side of the river and finds that \(\angle BAC \approx 82^\circ\) and \(\angle ABC \approx 52^\circ\). Approximate the distance from A to C.
Solution $$ \angle C = 180^\circ - 82^\circ - 52^\circ = 46^\circ$$ $$ \frac{sin C}{c} = \frac{sin B}{b}$$ $$ \frac{sin 46^\circ}{200} = \frac{sin 52^\circ}{b}$$ $$ b \cdot sin(46^\circ) = 200 \cdot sin(52^\circ)$$ $$ b = \frac{200 \cdot sin(52^\circ)}{sin(46^\circ)} \approx 219.09 \ ft$$
